Research

Split Ergativity in Kurdish Dialects

My primary research investigates the nature of split alignment systems in Sorani Kurdish varieties like Akoi. I explore how case and agreement patterns are conditioned by tense and aspect, arguing for a model where these features are dissociated in the syntactic structure. This work contributes to our understanding of the parameters governing ergativity and the architecture of the clause.

The Syntax-Morphology Interface

I am broadly interested in how syntactic structures are realized morphologically. My work adopts the framework of Distributed Morphology, analyzing phenomena like clitic placement, head movement, and morpheme ordering as post-syntactic operations that are sensitive to both hierarchical and linear relationships established in the syntax.

Information Structure and Clitic Placement

Another area of focus is the interaction between information structure (topic and focus) and the placement of clitics in Kurdish. I examine how the position of tense and agreement markers can be influenced by which element in the clause is focalized, providing insight into the structure of the left periphery.
